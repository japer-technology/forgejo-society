# 01 — Positioning

> Positioning statement, taglines, one-liners, and elevator pitches.

This document is the **canonical home** for the short-form language that
describes Forgejo Society to the outside world. Other surfaces — the root
`README.md`, web pages, slide decks, social posts — should link or quote
from here rather than coining their own variants.

---

## Positioning statement

> *To be drafted.* A single paragraph (3–5 sentences) that names the
> category Forgejo Society competes in, the audience it serves, the
> distinctive value it offers, and the proof that it can deliver.

Required elements when drafted:

- **Category.** What kind of thing Forgejo Society is (e.g. self-hosted
  cognitive forge, governed agent substrate).
- **Audience.** Who it is for, in concrete terms — see `02-audiences.md`.
- **Distinction.** What makes it unlike adjacent things (CI/CD platforms,
  agent frameworks, agent IDEs).
- **Proof.** Why the claim is credible — pointers to the specification,
  the plan, and the runtime.

## Taglines

The canonical project tagline is the line that already appears across the
repository:

> **The forge is the mind. The repo is an agency. The society thinks.**

Use it verbatim. Do not paraphrase it on external surfaces.

Secondary taglines may be drafted here for specific contexts (technical,
research, operator, federation). Each must be approved before use.

## One-liners

A one-liner is a single sentence that fits on a slide title, a social
banner, or the GitHub repository description. Candidates live in
[`messaging/one-liners.md`](messaging/one-liners.md) once that file is
created.

## Elevator pitches

Three lengths, all derived from the positioning statement:

- **15 seconds** — for casual conversation.
- **60 seconds** — for a conference hallway or a podcast intro.
- **3 minutes** — for a recorded short or an opening keynote slide.

Drafts live alongside this document once written; the canonical text for
each length is fixed here when approved.
