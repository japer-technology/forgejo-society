# 04 — Channels

> Distribution channels: web, social, talks, papers, federation.

A narrative without a channel is a private thought. This document is the
**catalogue of channels** through which Forgejo Society reaches each
audience, and the rules that govern each channel's use.

---

## Channel catalogue

For each channel, record:

- **Name** — the surface (e.g. project README, Mastodon, Fediverse blog,
  conference talks, federated forge announcements).
- **Owner** — the role responsible for posting and reviewing.
- **Cadence** — how often the channel is used.
- **Audience fit** — which segments from `02-audiences.md` it reaches.
- **Source-of-truth** — which document(s) under this folder supply the
  copy or assets.
- **Review** — the authority level required before publishing (per
  `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/01-governance/`).

### Initial channel set

- **Repository surfaces** — root `README.md`, pillar READMEs, release
  notes. Highest scrutiny; copy is drawn from `messaging/` and quoted,
  not paraphrased.
- **Project website** — long-form copy and link cards; sources from
  `web/`.
- **Federated social (Fediverse)** — short posts and threads; sources
  from `social/`.
- **Talks and conferences** — abstracts, slides, and demo scripts;
  sources from `talks/`.
- **Press and journalism** — boilerplate, FAQ, contact protocol; sources
  from `press/` and `07-press-kit.md`.
- **Sibling societies / federation** — announcements and invitations
  posted to federated peer repositories per the
  inter-repo communication plan in `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PLAN/13-inter-repo-communication.md`.

## Channels we deliberately avoid

Document, with reasons, the channels the project chooses not to use
(e.g. closed platforms, paid promotion, anything incompatible with
local-first and federation principles).

## Status

Scaffold. The initial channel set is stable; per-channel detail is
filled in as channels are activated.
