# 03 — Media Relations

> How we build and maintain relationships with journalists and publications.

This document describes how Forgejo Society relates to the press: who we
talk to, how we talk to them, and how those relationships are recorded.
The canonical media list lives in
[`media-list/`](media-list/README.md); the press-kit content itself lives
in [`../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/07-press-kit.md`](../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/07-press-kit.md)
and [`../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/press/`](../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/press/README.md).

---

## Audiences

We engage primarily with three kinds of media:

1. **Technical press** — publications and independent writers covering
   forge software, AI agents, governance, and self-hosting.
2. **Community publications** — newsletters, podcasts, and aggregators
   that serve the Forgejo, Gitea, AI agents, and broader free-software
   communities.
3. **Academic and research outlets** — venues that cover work on
   multi-agent systems, software engineering with AI, and cognitive
   architectures. Forgejo Society is not a research lab, but it is in
   conversation with this work.

We do **not** pursue general-interest or business press. The project is
not for that audience.

## Relationship model

- **Long-term, named contacts.** We maintain a small, named media list
  in [`media-list/`](media-list/) rather than a large mailing list.
- **Reciprocity.** When a publication covers the project, we link to
  their canonical version from
  [`coverage/`](coverage/README.md) and acknowledge it in any related
  announcement.
- **Transparency.** We do not offer paid placements, sponsored content,
  or undisclosed quid pro quo.

## Inquiry handling

External inquiries are routed through the contact protocol defined in
[`../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/07-press-kit.md`](../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/07-press-kit.md).
The publicity-specific rules are:

1. **Acknowledge within two business days**, even if the substantive
   reply takes longer.
2. **Record the inquiry.** Substantive inquiries are noted in
   `media-list/` (without exposing personal contact details).
3. **Stay in scope.** We answer questions about the project; we decline
   to speak on behalf of peer projects, employers of contributors, or
   the broader industry.
4. **Confirm in writing.** Anything quotable is confirmed in writing
   before publication, ideally by linking to a canonical document in this
   repository.

## Embargoes

- We accept embargoes only when they serve clarity (for example,
  coordinating an announcement with a sibling society).
- Embargoed material lives in a private branch or unpublished draft,
  never in the main branch.
- An embargo is honoured by the project as a whole; if it cannot be, we
  decline the embargo rather than break it.

## Exclusives

- We do not grant exclusives that would prevent us from publishing the
  underlying announcement in this repository at the same time.
- We may grant *first interview* arrangements where a publication is
  the first to speak with the project about an announcement that is
  itself public.

## Status

Scaffold. The media list is built up over time as relationships form.
