# 02 — Audiences

> Audience segments and tailored value propositions.

Promotion is wasted when it is undirected. This document identifies the
**concrete audiences** Forgejo Society speaks to, what each one cares
about, and the value proposition that meets them where they are.

---

## Primary audiences

For each audience, fill in:

- **Who they are** — role, context, the problem occupying their day.
- **Why they should care** — the specific gain or relief Forgejo Society
  offers them.
- **Proof points** — the documents, demos, or runtime artefacts that make
  the claim credible.
- **First action** — what we want them to do next (read X, run Y, file Z).

### Self-hosting maintainers

Operators of small fleets of Forgejo or similar forges who want more from
their forge than storage and CI.

### Agent-system researchers

People studying multi-agent architectures, governance, or
Society-of-Mind-style decompositions, looking for transparent substrates
to study or extend.

### Governance-minded engineers

Engineers who care about authority, audit, kill switches, and reviewable
behaviour, and who are wary of opaque agent platforms.

### Local-first and sovereignty advocates

Practitioners who want AI capabilities that run on hardware they own,
under licences they control.

### Forgejo and federation contributors

Members of the Forgejo and ForgeFed ecosystems who may federate with,
fork, or adopt parts of the project.

## Secondary audiences

Communities reached indirectly through primary audiences (e.g. educators,
journalists, students writing about agent governance).

## Anti-audiences

Audiences the project is **explicitly not** trying to win, listed here so
that copy does not drift toward them. Drafted in this document over time.

## Status

Scaffold. Audience names are stable; the per-audience detail is filled in
incrementally and reviewed before being quoted on external surfaces.
