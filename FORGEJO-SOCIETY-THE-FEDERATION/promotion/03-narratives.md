# 03 — Narratives

> Core narratives and key messages, with proof points.

A narrative is a sustained story the project tells about itself across
many surfaces over time. This document records the **small set** of
narratives Forgejo Society commits to, and the proof points that back
each one.

---

## Narrative inventory

For each narrative, capture:

- **Name** — a short handle used internally.
- **Statement** — the narrative in one or two sentences.
- **Why it matters** — the gap or misconception it addresses.
- **Proof points** — files, runtime behaviour, or governance artefacts
  that demonstrate the claim.
- **Audiences** — which segments from `02-audiences.md` it primarily
  serves.
- **Channels** — where it is told (see `04-channels.md`).

### Core narratives (initial set)

1. **The forge is the mind.**
   The CI/CD primitives a forge already provides — workflows, runners,
   jobs, branches, pull requests, labels, webhooks — are reinterpreted
   as primitives of cognition.

2. **Governance is the architecture.**
   Authority, critics, censors, kill switches, and audit trails are not
   bolted on; they are how the system thinks at all.

3. **Local-first cognition.**
   The substrate runs on hardware the project owns, with models and
   memory that do not require trusting a remote provider.

4. **Society, not service.**
   Forgejo Society is one society among many that can federate; the
   project is a member of an ecology, not a platform.

Each narrative will receive its own subsection here as it is fleshed out,
including the proof-point links that make the claim verifiable.

## Anti-narratives

Stories the project will **not** tell about itself, to keep the public
voice honest. Examples to be filled in:

- Not "AGI in your repo".
- Not "replace your engineers".
- Not "drop-in autonomous developer".
