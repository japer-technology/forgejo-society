## Operating principles

1. **Truth is a constraint, not a tactic.**
   - Publicity describes what is, or what has been formally decided. It
     does not describe wishes.
   - When uncertain, we under-claim rather than over-claim.

2. **The record is sacred.**
   - Announcements and statements, once published, are not silently
     edited. Material changes are issued as dated follow-ups.
   - Corrections are first-class artefacts and link back to the original.

3. **Relationships compound.**
   - We treat journalists, conference organisers, and peer projects as
     long-term collaborators, not as one-shot distribution channels.
   - We answer inquiries we cannot fully satisfy with a clear "no" and a
     pointer to whoever might be a better source.

4. **Quiet beats loud.**
   - We do not manufacture urgency. We do not pre-announce.
   - Cadence is determined by what the project has actually shipped, not
     by news cycles.

5. **No comparative attacks.**
   - Publicity describes Forgejo Society on its own terms. It does not
     define the project by negation of peer projects.
   - Where comparison is unavoidable (for example in interviews), it is
     factual and links to the peer project's own canonical sources.

6. **Federation-friendly.**
   - Where sibling societies or peer Forgejo deployments exist, we name
     them and link to them rather than absorbing their work into ours.

7. **Author of record.**
   - Every announcement, statement, and event entry names a human author
     of record, even when drafted with assistance.
   - The author of record is accountable for the claim, not for the
     phrasing.

8. **Pull-request first.**
   - All publicity materials are drafted, reviewed, and approved through
     pull requests in this repository.
   - Out-of-band publication (for example a tweet that has no draft in
     `announcements/` or `statements/`) is treated as an exception and
     is back-filled into the record at the earliest opportunity.

9. **Voice continuity.**
   - The publicity voice inherits the style guide in
     [`../FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/08-style-guide.md`](../promotion/08-style-guide.md).
   - Where publicity needs additional voice rules (for example for
     statements during incidents), they are added there, not duplicated
     here.

10. **Privacy by default.**
    - We do not publish contributors' personal contact details.
    - We do not name individuals in public statements without their
      consent, except in roles they have already publicly assumed in the
      project.

## Anti-patterns

The following are explicitly out of scope for this folder:

- Pre-announcements of features that are not yet specified or merged.
- Vanity metrics presented as project health.
- Press outreach that promises exclusivity we cannot honour.
- Any framing that obscures the project's substance â€” its specifications,
  governance, and runtime â€” behind generic language about "AI agents".
