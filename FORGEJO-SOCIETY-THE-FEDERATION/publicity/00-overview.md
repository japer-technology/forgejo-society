# 00 — Overview

> What publicity means for Forgejo Society, and the principles that govern it.

This document defines the **purpose, scope, and principles** of the
`FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PUBLICITY/` folder. Every other document in this folder
inherits from the principles stated here.

---

## Purpose

The publicity pillar exists to do four things, in this order:

1. **Make the project encounterable.** Ensure that when the world has
   reason to look for work like this, the project can be found, understood,
   and contacted with minimum friction.
2. **Build durable public relationships.** Cultivate honest relationships
   with journalists, community publications, conference organisers, and
   peer projects, so that coverage of the project is informed rather than
   speculative.
3. **Maintain an honest public record.** Keep an append-only, dated record
   of what the project has announced, where it has been covered, and what
   it has officially said — including corrections.
4. **Hold the project accountable to its own public claims.** Publicity is
   a form of constraint. Once something is announced, it is reviewable
   against the specification and the runtime.

## Scope

In scope:

- Strategy for *how* the project becomes publicly known and *to whom*.
- Announcements of milestones, releases, and significant changes.
- Media relationships, outreach, embargoes, and inquiries.
- Event participation: talks, demos, panels, podcasts.
- Logging and, where needed, correcting external coverage.
- Official public statements and crisis communications.
- Records of recognition the project receives.

Out of scope:

- Project decisions. Those live in `THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/01-governance/` and
  `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-IMPLEMENTATION/`.
- The project's outbound voice — taglines, narratives, visual identity,
  reusable copy. That lives in `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-PROMOTION/`.
- Teaching the project to readers already inside the repo. That is the job
  of `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INTRODUCTION/`.
- Operational instructions. Those live in `FORGEJO-SOCIETY-INSTALLATION/`.
- Runtime behaviour. That lives in `.forgejo-society/` and
  `REPO/forgejo-intelligence/`.

## Principles

1. **Substance first.** Publicity reports what already exists or has been
   formally decided. We do not announce capabilities the specification does
   not authorise or the runtime does not implement.
2. **Honesty over reach.** A small, accurate piece of coverage is more
   valuable than a large, misleading one. We will decline opportunities
   that require us to overstate the project.
3. **Append-only history.** Announcements, statements, and coverage logs
   are not silently rewritten. Corrections are added as dated follow-ups
   so the record of what we said, and when, remains intact.
4. **Reviewable like code.** All publicity materials change through pull
   requests, with the same authority levels as the rest of the project.
5. **One canonical source.** Each announcement, statement, and coverage
   entry has a single home in this folder. External surfaces link or quote.
6. **Federation-aware.** Forgejo Society is one society among many.
   Publicity should make space for sibling societies and inter-repo
   federation rather than claiming a monopoly on the idea.
7. **Calm register.** No hype, no manufactured urgency, no adversarial
   posture toward peers or critics. The project speaks in the voice it
   wants its readers to think in.
8. **Public-record discipline.** Anything said in the project's name in
   public should be discoverable in this folder, including the date, the
   author of record, and the governance decision (if any) it implements.
