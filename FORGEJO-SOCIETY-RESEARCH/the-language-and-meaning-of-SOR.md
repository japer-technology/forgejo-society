# The language and meaning of SOR

> A top-to-bottom language check for Society of Repo after the current
> Forge, Mind, Intelligence, Skills, and Labour layering.

The working sentence is strong because it uses verbs instead of an
equation:

> The Mind decides what matters. The Intelligence connects a repo to the
> loop. The Skills define what can be done. The forge records, governs,
> and settles the work, which is what makes the whole thing a Society.

That sentence should become the governing plain-language form. It
preserves the Society of Mind grounding better than "Society equals
Minds plus Intelligence plus Skills", because it says what each layer
does and keeps the binding mechanism visible. The Society is not the
sum of its parts. It is the governed interaction between them, recorded
in the forge.

## 1. The corrected sentence

Use this form when explaining the architecture to a human reader:

> The Mind decides what matters. The Intelligence connects a repo to the
> loop. The Skills define what can be done. Labour executes bounded work.
> The forge records, governs, and settles the work, which is what makes
> the whole thing a Society.

The only addition to the user's sentence is **Labour executes bounded
work**. Without that clause, the sentence correctly explains cognition
but leaves the execution layer implicit. The project now distinguishes
Intelligence from Labour clearly enough that the plain-language sentence
should do the same.

For shorter copy:

> Minds decide what matters. Intelligences connect repos. Skills define
> governed capability. Labour runs bounded work. The forge records,
> governs, and settles the loop.

For the most compressed public line:

> The forge makes Minds, Intelligences, Skills, and Labour accountable
> to one Society.

## 2. What SOR means

**SOR** means **Society of Repo**: a Git-native architecture in which
repositories become durable cognitive organs inside a governed ecology.
The word *Society* is load-bearing. It does not mean a collection, a
brand, or a group chat. It means a governed whole with:

- one identity scope;
- one authority registry;
- one memory spine;
- one cognitive loop;
- critics and censors;
- settlements;
- durable records of action and revision.

The central claim in
[`00-foundations/01-society-of-mind.md`](../THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/00-foundations/01-society-of-mind.md)
is that intelligence emerges from structured **interaction,
insulation, representation, and revision**. SOR is that claim rebuilt on
forge primitives:

| Society of Mind structure | SOR form |
| --- | --- |
| bounded agents | agencies, roles, and repos |
| frames | memory frames and activation contexts |
| K-lines | restored activation and inhibition patterns |
| critics | challenge weak reasoning or proposals |
| censors | enforce hard limits |
| hierarchy | summaries, assemblies, and directives |
| insulation | branches, scoped authority, and protected loops |
| representation | typed memory classes |
| credit assignment | recorded reinforcement and evaluation |
| introspection | recorded unknowns and blind spots |

SOR is therefore not a wrapper around a model. It is a governed forge
ecology in which cognition has address, history, review, inhibition,
settlement, memory, and evolution.

## 3. The nouns are not interchangeable

Most confusion comes from using nearby words as if they were the same
kind of thing. They are not.

| Term | Short meaning | What it does | What it must not be confused with |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Forge** | The owned substrate | Records, triggers, gates, reviews, runs, stores | A Society by itself |
| **Society** | The governed whole | Holds identity, authority, memory, settlement, and scope | A pile of tools |
| **Mind** | The unit of cleverness | Decides what matters under a purpose and authority level | A repo, a model, or a workflow |
| **Intelligence** | The repo connector | Installs the protocols by which a repo joins the loop | General cleverness |
| **Skill** | Constitutional capability | Defines what can be requested, attempted, criticised, and evaluated | A prompt or one-off tool call |
| **Labour** | Execution layer | Runs code, produces artefacts, commits results, reports back | Governance, reasoning, or intent |
| **Repo** | Unit of work | Holds code, memory, agency, service, or labour surface | The whole Society |
| **Critic** | Objection mechanism | Challenges weak proposals and shallow traces | A censor |
| **Censor** | Hard stop | Blocks non-negotiable violations | A critic |
| **Settlement** | Recorded resolution | Decides what happened and what may happen next | A mere output |

The most important distinction is this:

> A Mind may decide that a repo matters. An Intelligence lets that repo
> participate. A Skill says what kind of governed capability is in scope.
> Labour runs bounded execution. The forge makes every step reviewable.

## 4. Layer check, from bottom to top

### 4.1 Forge

The forge is the substrate: repositories, issues, pull requests,
branches, labels, workflow runs, permissions, runners, commits, reviews,
and releases.

The correct verb for the forge is not *thinks* in everyday explanatory
copy. The sharper verbs are:

- records;
- triggers;
- gates;
- reviews;
- runs;
- stores;
- settles;
- preserves.

The project can still say "the forge is the mind" as a thesis, because
the Society of Repo foundation gives that phrase a specific meaning:
the forge is the durable cognitive substrate in which settlements,
memory, conflict, and revision live. But in explanatory prose, the
sentence should show the mechanism:

> The forge records, governs, and settles the work.

That line keeps the substrate visible without implying that the forge
alone supplies purpose.

### 4.2 Society

The Society is the outer container. It is created and governed. It has
one constitution, one authority registry, one memory spine, and one
identity scope.

The Society is not simply "where the agents are". It is the condition
under which parts can cite one another, constrain one another, remember
one another, and revise one another. If those conditions are absent,
there may be automation, but there is not yet a Society in the SOR
sense.

The best verb for Society is **binds**:

> The Society binds Minds, Intelligences, Skills, Labour, critics,
> censors, memory, and settlement into one governed ecology.

### 4.3 Mind

The Mind is the unit of cleverness inside a Society. The composition
model says a Mind has a purpose, an authority level, memory of its own,
and the right to decide which repos it needs in order to be that Mind.

The user's phrase is therefore right:

> The Mind decides what matters.

But the sentence should be read narrowly. A Mind decides what matters
**inside its authority and purpose**. It does not make itself legitimate
by wanting something. Legitimacy still comes from the Society's
constitution, authority registry, approval gates, critics, censors, and
settlements.

Better expanded form:

> The Mind decides what matters within its purpose, authority, and
> memory.

### 4.4 Intelligence

Forgejo Intelligence is not "intelligence" in the loose sense. It is a
repository-native runtime and incorporation mechanism. It lives inside
the repository it operates on, responds to Forgejo events, validates
guardrails, runs the agent, posts through the Forgejo API, and commits
state back to the repository.

The composition model is stricter:

> An Intelligence is the thing you manually drop into a repo to make
> that repo part of a Society.

So the user's phrase is correct:

> The Intelligence connects a repo to the loop.

That is better than saying "the Intelligence thinks". The Mind is the
unit of cleverness. The Intelligence is the installed connector that
gives a repo identity, event handling, authority hooks, memory hooks,
and participation in the cognitive loop.

Use:

- "Intelligence connects a repo to the loop."
- "Intelligence incorporates a repo."
- "Intelligence gives the repo the protocols it needs to participate."

Avoid:

- "Intelligence is the Mind."
- "Intelligence is the Society."
- "Intelligence is any model call."

### 4.5 Skill

Skills are constitutional capabilities, not prompts. The skills
catalogue says they operate across domains: internet operations, local
document management, software research and development, business
operations, personal operations, and meta-administration.

The user's phrase is right:

> The Skills define what can be done.

The stronger form is:

> The Skills define what can be done, under which authority, with which
> critics, censors, evaluation rules, and memory effects.

A Skill is not just an action. It is an authorised pattern of work that
can be requested, activated, criticised, inhibited, settled, remembered,
and improved.

### 4.6 Labour

Labour is the execution layer. Its own README says it does not govern,
reason, or hold intent. It runs code, receives tasks, produces artefacts,
commits results, and reports back.

This makes Labour distinct from both Intelligence and Skill:

- Intelligence connects the repo to the cognitive loop.
- Skill defines a governed capability.
- Labour executes bounded work when a permitted call reaches it.

The language should not let Labour become a hidden second Mind. Labour
has no purpose of its own. It is a controlled execution surface.

Use:

> Labour runs bounded work and reports the result back to the loop.

Avoid:

> Labour decides.

### 4.7 Critic and censor

Critics and censors are what keep "can do" from becoming "may do
anything".

A critic challenges. A censor blocks. That distinction should stay
sharp:

- A critic may say: the evidence is weak, the scope is wrong, the risk
  is understated, the reasoning trace is shallow.
- A censor may say: this violates authority, privacy, cloud-egress,
  payment, compliance, or another hard rule; stop.

The Society becomes governable only when proposals pass through both
forms of constraint.

### 4.8 Settlement

Settlement is the point at which competing proposals, objections,
blocks, evidence, and authority checks become a recorded resolution.
This is why the user's final clause works:

> The forge records, governs, and settles the work, which is what makes
> the whole thing a Society.

The settlement is what prevents the system from being only a stream of
outputs. It gives the Society a durable answer to:

- what was proposed;
- what objected;
- what was blocked;
- what was approved;
- what changed;
- what should be remembered;
- what should be revised next time.

## 5. The cognitive loop, rewritten with the new sentence

The Society of Repo loop can be read through the sentence like this:

| Loop step | Layer language |
| --- | --- |
| stimulus | The forge receives an issue, event, commit, webhook, timer, or file change. |
| perception | Intelligence normalises the event into a form the repo can process. |
| frame selection | The Mind and memory choose what kind of situation this is. |
| activation | K-lines, analogy, budgets, and relevant Skills wake. |
| agency response | A Mind, through an intelligent repo or agency, proposes work. |
| criticism | Critics challenge the proposal. |
| inhibition and censorship | Censors block forbidden paths; inhibitors narrow weak ones. |
| settlement | The Society records what is allowed, rejected, or deferred. |
| action | Labour or another authorised surface executes bounded work. |
| outcome | Artefacts, comments, commits, releases, or reports return. |
| memory | Results become episodes, facts, procedures, frames, K-lines, failures, or decisions. |
| credit assignment | The Society records which layer helped or harmed. |
| evolution | Skills, critics, censors, frames, and agencies are revised. |

This reading matters because it prevents a false collapse:

- the Mind is not the whole loop;
- the Intelligence is not the whole loop;
- the Skill is not the whole loop;
- Labour is not the whole loop;
- the forge alone is not the whole loop.

The Society is the governed loop made durable.

## 6. The Mind–Brain–Body check

[`06-mind-brain-body.md`](../THE-SOCIETY-OF-REPO/00-foundations/06-mind-brain-body.md)
separates three layers:

| Layer | SOR meaning | Language rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Body | forge, runners, storage, tools, network, external services | Say what executes and where it runs. |
| Brain | models, classifiers, retrieval indexes, pattern matchers | Say what predicts, retrieves, classifies, or generates. |
| Mind | settlements, critics, censors, constitutions, self-ideals, reasoning traces | Say what authorises, judges, remembers, and revises. |

The proposed sentence passes this check if Labour is included:

- **Mind decides what matters** — mind layer.
- **Intelligence connects a repo to the loop** — body/coordination
  doorway plus protocol binding.
- **Skills define what can be done** — mind layer, expressed as
  constitutional capability.
- **Labour executes bounded work** — body layer.
- **The forge records, governs, and settles** — body substrate plus mind
  governance made durable.

The important warning from the decomposition is that technical
possibility is not legitimacy. A runner can execute. A model can
generate. An Intelligence can receive an event. None of that means the
action is authorised. Authority belongs to the mind layer and is
settled through the Society.

## 7. Forge, Intelligence, Skills, and Labour as doorways

The current project language can be stabilised by treating the runtime
parts as doorways:

| Doorway | What enters | What comes out |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Forge** | Events, files, permissions, workflow runs | durable objects, reviews, settlements, history |
| **Intelligence** | repo events and human commands | normalised participation in the cognitive loop |
| **Skills** | governed requests for capability | proposed work under authority, critics, censors, and evaluation |
| **Labour** | approved calls to executable units | artefacts, logs, commits, and result envelopes |
| **Memory** | outcomes and settlements | frames, K-lines, procedures, failures, decisions, and reusable context |

This doorway language is useful because it keeps each mechanism bounded.
No doorway is the whole Society. The Society is the architecture that
connects them under governance.

## 8. What changes in the top-level story

The earlier story was:

> Forges let Societies emerge when Minds, Intelligences, and Skills act
> together as one.

That remains correct, but it should now be read with Labour explicit:

> Forges let Societies emerge when Minds decide purposes,
> Intelligences connect repos, Skills define governed capabilities, and
> Labour executes bounded work under settlement.

This is not a new thesis. It is a refinement of the existing one.
Labour was already present as execution, but the language now gives it
a proper place so that Intelligence does not accidentally absorb every
meaning.

The top-level story becomes:

1. **Forge** supplies the owned substrate.
2. **Society** supplies governance and identity.
3. **Mind** supplies purpose and attention.
4. **Intelligence** incorporates repos into the loop.
5. **Skills** supply authorised capability.
6. **Critics and censors** constrain proposals.
7. **Settlement** records what is allowed.
8. **Labour** executes bounded work.
9. **Memory** preserves the result.
10. **Evolution** revises the ecology.

That is the complete Society of Repo sentence in expanded form.

## 9. Recommended canonical wording

### Full explanatory paragraph

> In Society of Repo, the Mind decides what matters within its purpose
> and authority. Intelligence connects a repository to the cognitive
> loop. Skills define the governed capabilities that may be invoked.
> Labour executes bounded work when a permitted call reaches it. The
> forge records, governs, criticises, censors, settles, and remembers
> the result. That durable governed loop is what makes the parts a
> Society.

### Short paragraph

> The Mind decides what matters. Intelligence connects the repo. Skills
> define governed capability. Labour runs bounded work. The forge
> records and settles the loop.

### One-line form

> Society of Repo is the governed loop where Minds choose, Intelligences
> connect, Skills authorise capability, Labour executes, and the forge
> records the settlement.

### What not to say

Do not say:

> Society = Minds + Intelligence + Skills.

That language hides the binding mechanism and makes unlike things look
additive.

Do not say:

> Intelligence is the Society.

Intelligence is the incorporation mechanism. The Society is the
governed whole.

Do not say:

> Labour thinks.

Labour executes. The Mind, critics, censors, settlements, memory, and
authority registry govern the work.

## 10. Final check

The user's sentence is substantially right. The corrected version is:

> The Mind decides what matters. The Intelligence connects a repo to the
> loop. The Skills define what can be done. Labour executes bounded
> work. The forge records, governs, and settles the work, which is what
> makes the whole thing a Society.

This should be treated as the plain-language grammar for SOR.

It is faithful to Minsky because it does not locate intelligence in one
part. It is faithful to Society of Repo because it names the governed
loop, the forge substrate, the cognitive roles, and settlement. It is
faithful to Forgejo Society because it keeps sovereignty structural:
the work happens on the owned forge, under files, review, runners,
permissions, memory, and git history.
