# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Forgejo Society follows the Contributor Covenant 2.1 as its baseline
expectation for community conduct. We pledge to make participation in this
project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility and apologising to those affected by mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its community.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

---

## Project Context

Forgejo Society is a repository about governed agency, review, memory, and
runtime authority. That subject matter requires particular care.

Contributors must not use project spaces to:

- Encourage abuse, surveillance, coercion, or harassment.
- Request help exposing credentials, private records, or personal information.
- Present planned runtime behaviour as existing behaviour.
- Pressure maintainers to weaken the warning, compliance, governance, or
  approval-gate documents.
- Obscure whether work is proposed by a human contributor, an assistant, or an
  internal agency operating under the Society of Repo model.

Disagreement is expected. Personal attack, coercion, and bad-faith pressure are
not part of the review process.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull
requests, discussions, reviews, repository-hosted documents, project chats,
public events, and private communications made in the context of project work.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in
public spaces.

---

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behaviour to the project maintainers through a private
maintainer contact channel. Do not open a public issue to report conduct that
identifies a person without their consent.

Reports should include, where possible:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved or affected.
- Any links, screenshots, timestamps, or other context that will help the
  maintainers review the report.

Maintainers will respect the privacy and safety of the reporter and any affected
people. Reports will be handled with care, proportionate confidentiality, and a
focus on stopping harm rather than creating spectacle.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable
behaviour. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code,
documentation, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not align
with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation by a
contributor whose behaviour is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, harmful,
or disruptive to the project.

Enforcement actions may include:

- A private clarification or warning.
- A request to amend or withdraw a comment, issue, pull request, or public
  statement.
- Temporary restriction from one or more project spaces.
- Permanent removal from project spaces.
- Escalation to platform administrators, venue staff, or legal authorities when
  required for safety.

When an enforcement decision affects governance, runtime authority, or project
settlement, maintainers should record the decision in the appropriate governance
or memory surface without exposing private details unnecessarily.
